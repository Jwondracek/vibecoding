Lawyer Due Diligence for Cloud & Third-Party Tech Vendors

A 50-state map of bar ethics opinions on a lawyer's duty to vet technology vendors for confidentiality, security, backup, breach notice, and data retrieval. The benchmark is the three-part checklist in Washington Advisory Op. 2215 (2012), quoted in Ohio Op. 2017-5 and carried forward by ABA Formal Op. 512 (2024). Select any state for its opinion.

How to read the colors. Darkest tiles adopt the granular three-part vendor checklist; mid tiles list some but not all elements; pale tiles endorse only a general "reasonable efforts" standard; gray tiles have no dedicated cloud opinion and are governed by their adopted versions of Rule 1.1 cmt. [8] (technology competence), Rule 1.6(c) (reasonable efforts to safeguard), and Rule 5.3 (supervising nonlawyer vendors).

Caveats. North Dakota Op. 99-03 is verified by secondary authority only; Missouri publishes informal-opinion summaries, not full text; Arizona Op. 09-04 issued under prior committee authority; Wisconsin Op. EF-15-01 is member-gated. Several gray-tile states have adjacent opinions on email, virtual practice, data breach, or generative AI that impose a comparable duty.

Citations verified to exist, to stand for the stated proposition, and for currentness. Survey compiled June 2026. Tile grid is a geographic approximation, not a precise map.
